Bellary Sheep

Bellary

The Bellary sheep is a medium-sized Indian breed of sheep native to the districts of Bellary and Davanagere and the adjoining areas of Haveri and Chitradurga districts of Karnataka.

Origin and Distribution:

The breed is primarily found in the districts of Bellary and Davanagere, and the adjoining areas of Haveri and Chitradurga districts in Karnataka.

Sheep found to the north of the Tungabhadra River are called "Deccani" and those to the south of it, "Bellary".

In farmers' flocks lambing percentage is 80–85% and the litter size is generally single. Breeding is mostly pure.

Rams are selected on the basis of size. The animals are primarily maintained for meat. The ewes are milked for home consumption.

The animals are shorn twice a year in June and December/January. The average annual fleece weight is 600 gms with an average fibre diameter and density of 59 μ and 350 cm2 respectively, and a medullation percentage of 43. According to the 18th Livestock Census 2007, the number of Bellary sheep in the country is 1,754,507.

Physical Characteristics:

  • Size: Medium-sized.
  • Body Color: Ranges from white through various combinations of white and black to complete black.
  • Horns: Most rams have horns, while ewes are polled (hornless).
  • Ears: Medium-sized, flat, and droop.
  • Tail: Thin and short.
  • Fleece: Coarse, hairy, and open.
  • Wool: There is no wool on the belly and legs.

Reproduction

Most of the flock were puredbred.Rams are selected on the basis of body size and conformation. Body length and height at wither are given due weightage. Some of the farmers preferred rams with thicker and bigger horns.

The rans start breeding at the age of 15-18 months. At this age, the average body weight of selected rams was 40.2kg.

The average estimated body weight at birth, 3, 6 and 12 months of age under the feild management conditions were 2.01, 14.1,19.0 and 25.7kg in male lambs and 2.20, 13.3, 17.6 and 23.2kg in female.
