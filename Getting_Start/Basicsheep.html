Basic Information About Sheep

General Knowledge & Facts About Sheep

Let's start out with some general sheep information and answers to common questions about sheep as a species and type of livestock.

On this page we've assembled some of our articles which fall into a general knowledge category. These include answers to questions about sheep horns, tails and other physical characteristics.

We'll also share answers to common questions about what types of food you can (and can't) feed to sheep as treats, why sheep look the way they do, and information about the history of sheep as a species and how they came to be used on farms around the world..

Sheep as a Species

Sheep (called Ovis aries) are farm animals that chew their food more than once. They are soft, woolly animals that people raise for many uses. There are more than a billion sheep around the world. Most sheep today came from wild sheep called mouflon.

Farmers keep sheep for their wool (hair), meat, milk, and skin. Some sheep are also used as pets or to keep grass short in big open spaces.

  • Wool: Used to make clothes like sweaters, socks, and blankets.
  • Meat: Lamb and mutton are two kinds of meat from sheep.
  • Milk: Used to make cheese and yogurt.
  • Skin: Used to make leather products.

Taking care of sheep is called sheep farming. It is common all over the world. Sheep can live in many kinds of weather and land.

What Do Sheep Eat?

Sheep mostly eat grass, hay, and plants. They need fresh, clean food every day. Giving sheep the right food helps them stay healthy and strong.

  • They need enough food — not too little or too much.
  • The food should be clean and fresh.
  • The food should be balanced with all nutrients they need.

When Do Sheep Eat and How Much Space Do They Need?

Sheep eat for about seven hours a day if there's enough grass. They like to eat in the morning and evening when it's cool.

Sheep move in groups called flocks. They walk together while eating and rest together too.

A small group of sheep needs a big open space (called a pasture) to walk and eat freely. One acre of land can usually feed 2 to 4 sheep.

Different Types of Sheep

There are many kinds (called breeds) of sheep around the world. Each has special qualities:

  • Merino: Very soft wool, popular for clothing.
  • East Friesian: Known for giving lots of milk.
  • Suffolk: Grows fast and gives good meat.
  • Deccani, Bannur, Bellary: Common breeds in India.

How to Take Care of Sheep

  • Give them clean water every day.
  • Keep their shelter dry and safe from rain and wild animals.
  • Check their health often — call a vet if needed.
  • Shear (cut their wool) at least once a year to keep them comfortable.

Fun Facts About Sheep

  • Sheep can remember up to 50 faces — both sheep and humans!
  • Lambs (baby sheep) can walk just minutes after they are born.
  • Sheep have great hearing and can even recognize voices.
  • They can see behind themselves without turning their heads.
