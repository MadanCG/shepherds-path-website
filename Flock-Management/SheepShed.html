Housing of Sheep

General Considerations for Sheep Housing

Low cost house

Open type housing with run space

  • Provision of simple sheds with low-cost materials is sufficient for sheep housing.
  • Sheds with mud floors are suitable except in areas with high rainfall.
  • Construct sheds in an elevated area to prevent water stagnation.
  • Fodder trees around the shed can act as a source of feed.
  • Clean drinking water must be available.
  • Proper ventilation is essential for sheds.
  • Walls of the shed should be free from cracks and holes.
  • Floors should be firm and capable of absorbing water.
  • Types of sheds depend on the rearing system.
  • Open-type housing with covered areas and run space is ideal.
  • The run space should be covered by chain links.
  • East-west orientation with good ventilation is preferred.
  • Thatched roofs are cost-effective, but asbestos sheets provide durability.
  • Gable roofing is generally preferred for better airflow.
  • Height of sheds should be around 2.5m, with ridge height at 3.5m.
  • Separate feeders and water troughs should be provided.

Types of Sheds

  • Different sheds are required for various age groups.

General flock shed

  • The flock shed shall be used for housing ewes or does kept for breeding purpose.
  • shed shall be 15m (l) x 4m (w) x 3 m (h) and can accommodate not more than 60 ewes or does.
  • The shed should be three metre high and should have brick-on-edge floor.
  • In low lying and heavy rainfall areas, the floors should preferably be elevated and in temperate regions they may be made of strong wood.

Lambing/Kidding Shed

  • These sheds shall be used as maternity rooms for pregnant ewe or doe and are housed individually in these sheds.
  • The shed shall be 1.5m(l)×1.2m(w)×3.0m(h), a manger for holding feed and hay and a bucket for keeping water shall be provided in the shed.
  • These sheds shall be made draught free.
  • In cold climates some warming device, like a room heater shall be fixed in maternity pens, so that new borns are protected from cold during winter.
